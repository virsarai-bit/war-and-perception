Government Policy

When Perception Becomes Law

Primary Document  ·  Exhibit Source

Executive Order 13769

"Protecting the Nation From Foreign Terrorist Entry Into the United States." Federal Register 82, no. 20 (2017): 8977–8982

Banned citizens of seven Muslim-majority countries (Iran, Iraq, Libya, Somalia, Sudan, Syria, Yemen) from entering the U.S., all active or recent sites of U.S. military operations. Invoked 9/11 as justification. None of the 9/11 hijackers came from any of the seven countries listed.

03

Sources

Chicago style. Annotated with the BEAM framework: Background, Exhibit, Argument, Method.

Background

Bridge Initiative. The Super Survey. Georgetown University, 2015.

Synthesizes over 400 surveys (1993–2014) to establish that negative attitudes toward Muslims in the United States correlate with periods of U.S. military activity, not merely reactions to specific attacks.

Argument

Kumar, Deepa. Islamophobia and the Politics of Empire. Haymarket Books, 2012.

Argues that Islamophobia is a structural phenomenon produced and maintained by U.S. imperial interests, not a free-floating prejudice. Provides the causal mechanism linking military engagement to domestic discrimination.

Argument

Little, Douglas. "Orientalism and the Middle East." The International History Review 26, no. 1 (2004): 110–135.

Shows that Orientalist assumptions were not merely cultural artifacts but active forces shaping U.S. Cold War and post-Cold War policy decisions in the Middle East.

Method / Theory

Said, Edward W. Orientalism. Pantheon Books, 1978.

The theoretical lens for the entire project. Said argues the West constructs an imagined "Orient" defined by inferiority and danger as a justification for domination, not as a description of reality.

Exhibit

Pew Research Center. U.S. Muslims Concerned About Their Place in Society. 2017.

Primary survey data: approximately half of Muslim Americans experienced discrimination in a single year; over half say the post-9/11 environment has materially worsened their daily lives.

Exhibit

Trump, Donald. Executive Order 13769. Federal Register 82, no. 20 (2017): 8977–8982.

Primary government document analyzed as an exhibit: shows how the militarized framing of Muslim people in foreign policy translates directly into domestic law. The order invoked 9/11 to restrict citizens of countries with no connection to the attacks.
